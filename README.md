## About

I'm a second-year Electrical Engineering student at **National Taiwan University** working on **learning-based robotics**. I'm drawn to a single question: how can robots acquire manipulation and control skills *efficiently* — from a handful of demonstrations, from their own interaction, and increasingly from large vision-language priors?

I'm currently a research intern at **Learning Robots** (Paris-Saclay), and a member of the **NTU Robot Learning Lab** (`NTU-RL2025-02`). I care as much about *why* a method works as about getting it to run.

## Research Interests

- **Imitation & online learning** — query-efficient learning from demonstrations, and recovering from the compounding errors that plague behavior cloning.
- **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models** — grounding perception and language into low-level robot action.
- **Reinforcement learning** — sample-efficient policy learning for continuous control.
